ABOUT THE DREAM BIG DESIGNATHON

The annual Dream Big Designathon, organised by Space Matrix since its first edition in 2019 in conjunction with Singapore's Temasek Polytechnic School of Design, has since seen 14 shortlisted student finalists (from two Year 2 batches) battle it out for thousands of dollars in prize money and four internship places with the Singapore studio.

Through the past two editions of the Dream Big Designathon, the students were able to gain invaluable real-world exposure that came from working on a tight industry-relevant brief set by Space Matrix, in a competitive environment, guided by some of our most inspiring practitioners / designers. The students had to design a workplace that had a front end customer experience (CX) space and a back end employee coworking space (EX), in line with how workspaces are evolving, choosing from either the online fashion retail, tech, food services or wind energy industries to explore their design ideas.

In 2020 the Dream Big Designathon was expanded and launched in India in conjunction with Sir JJ College of Architecture, one of the top architectural schools in the country.

For the 2021/2022 edition in Singapore, Space Matrix has partnered with the generous and esteemed financial institution CITI to expand the Dream Big Designathon to Singapore Polytechnic and Nanyang Polytechnic as well, so as to create a greater, more meaningful impact on a wider generation of young student talent.

PROJECT BRIEF

Design an Innovation Lab that will be dedicated to the pursuit of new ideas, products and services for Singapore’s young innovators. The Innovation Lab will be a place to fast-track and accelerate new ideas, to understand new market trends, and to develop new solutions and processes for business and customers. In essence, this is your very own R&D centre.

The overall objective is to create a dynamic creative incubation space that offers a host of services, tools and solutions to help innovators kick-start, launch and succeed in realising their business ideas together.

First, establish the industry that you would like to design your Innovation Lab for, and then decide and determine the types of activities and goals that you would like to create that are essential for innovating. Your Innovation Lab can function in many different ways, it can be operated by a well-known company, or it can be operated independently.

Your soon-to-be Innovation Lab will be situated in the CapitaSpring building in the heart of the CBD in Singapore, and your exciting new space should be designed contextually relevant to the surrounding areas. It must be an experiential, beautifully designed space, enticing employees and potential clients to come together to work, innovate and collaborate together to bring to the market new and exciting opportunities together.
